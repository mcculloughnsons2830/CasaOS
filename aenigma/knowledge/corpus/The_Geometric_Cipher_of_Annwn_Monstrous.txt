We posit that the bardic "Software" (established in Cad Goddeu via the Solar Prime 29) is designed

the modular j-invariant and the ﬁfth harmonic partial sum (H5 = 137/60) provide the necessary
mathematical constraints to validate the model, linking the "Seven Survivors" to the periods of the
Periodic Table and the "One Spark" to the extracted Singularity.

Introduction: The Convergence of Mythos and Mathematics
The medieval Welsh poem Preiddeu Annwn, preserved in the 14th-century Book of Taliesin (Peniarth
MS 2), has long stood as the pons asinorum of Celtic scholarship. Composed of sixty cryptic lines,
the text describes a harrowing raid led by King Arthur aboard the ship Prydwen into the Otherworld
(Annwn). For centuries, predominant hermeneutic frameworks have viewed the “Caers” (Fortresses)
as vague metaphors or localized Iron Age hillforts. However, these linear interpretations collapse
when confronted with the poem’s rigid, recurring, and highly speciﬁc numerical assertions.
This paper addresses a critical lacuna in the current discourse: the identiﬁcation of the “missing”
mathematics that rigorously bridges the geometric intuition of the Grant Polytope Framework with
the fundamental constants of physics. We posit that the rigorous bridge lies in the domain of Mon-
strous Moonshine—the deep, non-trivial connection between the Monster Group (M) and modular
functions (the j-invariant).
Why Monstrous Moonshine and the Harmonic Series Are Structural Constraints
The introduction of Monstrous Moonshine and harmonic-series arithmetic in this analysis is not in-
tended as an appeal to numerological resonance, but as a proposal of necessary mathematical con-
straints if the Unity Harmonica framework is to be non-accidental. Monstrous Moonshine is the only
rigorously established mathematical context in which large, speciﬁc integers arise from the interac-

series, H5 = 137/60. This identity establishes 60 as a mathematically necessary denominator for 137
within harmonic arithmetic, independent of interpretation.
