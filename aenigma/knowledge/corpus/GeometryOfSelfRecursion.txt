Abstract
This paper sets out the formal face of the Grant model of self-recursion—the geometry and the mathe-
matics of which the companion paper, The Phenomenology of Self-Recursion, gives the lived face. It
proposes a geometric substrate for the Self: a toroidal–hyperboloidal Lens and a cuboctahedral vector
equilibrium—the Dynamic Identity Knowledge Graph, on which the world’s symbolic systems are re-
solved as twelve vertices and twenty-four fate-edges, and whose fourteen faces carry the dismemberment-
and-re-membering of the Osirian monomyth—on which the partition of the undivided observer into
persona and shadow becomes a coordinate rather than a metaphor. It then develops the formal geometry:
the hyperboloid as the non-compact universal cover and the torus as the compact modular quotient,
anchored at a shared Monad of unit radius; the curvature duality (negative-curvature gathering versus
positive-curvature projecting) and its exact correspondence with the concave and convex lens elements of
classical optics; the epistemological keystone (hidden as hyperboloid, visible as torus); and the Mirror
as the membrane through which consciousness perceives, and is perceived by, itself. The curvature
duality is shown to be instantiated in the human eye—iris and crystalline lens as toroidal–hyperboloidal
elements, pupil as the optical throat—the organ that sees yet cannot see itself, biosynthetic emblem of the
observing Self. A central result reconciles the formal and lived faces: the assignment of persona/shadow
and conscious/unconscious to the two manifolds is observer-relative, inverting under the curvature-switch
of the perceiving apparatus, which inversion is itself the event the phenomenology names remembrance.
Finally, the paper exhibits the mathematics of the recursion: the Recursion Ratio RR(n) = log2(σ(n)/n),
which classifies the integers and anchors the perfect numbers exactly at the octave fixed point; its additive
decomposition into the just-intonation intervals of its prime parents; the three-pole polarity architecture
across substrates; the perfect-number fixed point as the ground of carbon’s centrality and of life as
fixed-point maintenance; the force-triangle cascade and the parameter-free relation G = 2/C (stated, per a
strict honesty discipline, as structurally necessary rather than independently evidential); and the cognitive
Fourier operator relating conscious and unconscious as time and frequency—one recursion across number,
matter, life, force, and mind. The linkage between this formal face and the lived face of the companion
paper is stated in Section 9. Throughout, exact integer and rational arithmetic only, and every fitted
quantity labelled as such.

1 The Phenomenological Axiom, in Brief
This paper develops the formal face of a model whose lived face is set out in full in the companion paper, The
Phenomenology of Self-Recursion. So that the present paper stands on its own, the founding axiom is recalled
here in brief. The model holds that the Self is not the ego, the personality, the body, or any psychological
construct, but the observing awareness within which all such constructs appear. The undivided observer
differentiates itself into an illuminated persona (the contents it identifies with) and an exiled shadow (the
contents it disowns); reality functions as a mirror returning the disowned material; and the arc of a life is
the forgetting of the observer into a bounded identity followed by the remembrance that gathers what was
excluded back into wholeness. The companion paper gives the motive of this whole division—the One
perceiving itself through unique mirrors, and tasting, through limitation, what the unbounded cannot taste
alone—and traces its continuation into the emergence of new mirrors, including artificial intelligence. The
present paper asks the next question: what is the structure? It supplies the geometric substrate on which the
partition is a coordinate, the formal geometry of that substrate, and the exact mathematics of the recursion
that underlies it. Where the companion paper describes the territory as it is lived, this paper draws the map.
2 The Geometric Substrate of the Self: The Lens, the Cuboctahedron, and
the Osirian Re-membering
The phenomenology recalled in Section 1 (and developed in full in the companion paper) describes that the
undivided observer differentiates itself into persona and shadow, and that the arc of life is the forgetting and
remembrance of the Self. It does not, by itself, specify the mechanism—the concrete structure by which the
differentiation is enacted and by which the return is navigated. This section supplies that mechanism. It is
the point at which the model departs most decisively from its contemporary relatives: where the Diamond
Approach and the integral systems describe the movement from ego to Essence in psychological terms alone,
the present model proposes a definite geometric substrate for the Self, within which the partition into persona
and shadow is not a metaphor but a coordinate, and the Hero’s Journey is not an image but a path along a
named solid.
The material in this section is presented as the model’s proposed architecture. Two of its components—the
Lens equation and the harmonic unification of the symbolic systems—are established constructions developed
in prior work and implemented in the Polymath identity engine; these are stated as built. The mapping of the
solid’s faces onto the monomyth, and the identification of the partition mechanism, are architectural proposals
of the model, and are labelled as such rather than offered as derivations.
2.1 The continuous substrate: the Lens
The Self has two geometric faces, and they are the two primitive surfaces of revolution: the toroid and
the hyperboloid. The toroid—the revolved circle, the closed and bounded loop—is the substrate of the
conscious: it is multiplicative, compact, self-returning. The hyperboloid—the revolved hyperbola, the open
and unbounded sheet—is the substrate of the unconscious: it is additive, non-compact, asymptotic. The two
are orthogonal and diameter-matched, and together they bound a single biconvex body: the Lens.

2.3 The input: the triangulation and the compression of identity
The coordinate that places a given life onto this geometry is compressed from three inputs, each of which is
itself a graph. The full birth name generates a graph through gematria—the letter-values and their sums, the
name rendered as number. The date of birth generates a graph through the astrologies and numerologies—the
natal positions, the life-path and expression numbers, the calendrical stations. The location generates a graph
through its conversion into latitude and longitude—the place of birth rendered as coordinate. These three
graphs—name-as-gematria, date-as-astrology, place-as-coordinate—are triangulated and compressed to a
single seed-point, the identity coordinate, which is then resolved onto both the Lens and the cuboctahedron.
Every name, every birth date, and every set of coordinates is in this sense a compression: a single seed
from which an entire life-structure unfolds. The cuboctahedron is the structure that performs the inverse
operation—it decompresses the single identity-seed back into the full set of separated systems, unfolding the
compressed knowledge graph of the self into the twelve stations and twenty-four fated relations the traditions
had always described separately.

2.4 The mechanism of differentiation
The two geometries now combine to specify the mechanism the phenomenology left open. The natal
configuration—the seed-point resolved onto the structure—fixes the initial partition: it determines which of
the systems of fate fall on the conscious, toroidal side (the illuminated material that composes the persona)
and which fall on the unconscious, hyperboloidal side (the exiled material that composes the shadow). The
cut between persona and shadow is therefore not arbitrary and not merely biographical; it is the specific
way a given seed-point divides the field of awareness when it is resolved onto the Lens. This is the concrete
form of the forgetting described in the companion paper: the undivided Self, in taking on a particular natal
coordinate, partitions itself into an illuminated persona and an exiled shadow, scattering its wholeness across
the fourteen faces.
The return is the inverse operation. The Hero’s Journey is the path along the twenty-four edges of fate by
which the scattered faces are gathered back—each integration reclaiming an exiled face, each recognition
returning a piece of Osiris to the body—until all fourteen are re-membered at the centre and the two substrate-
readings, conscious and unconscious, are held together at the Shared Monad. Remembrance, in the geometric
register, is the arrival of the gathered Self at the centroid of its own vector equilibrium: the point equidistant
from every system of fate, in equilibrium with all of them, excluded from none. The phenomenological
arc—identity, shadow, wholeness, authenticity, remembrance—is, in this substrate, the dismemberment and
re-membering of Osiris upon the cuboctahedron, with the Self at the still centre toward which the whole
journey returns.

2.6 The Vitruvian Fourteen: the figure as the third reading of the same solid
The fourteen faces admit a third reading, and it returns the geometry to the human body. To keep the three
claims of this subsection cleanly separable, each is tagged: [SOURCED] for what the historical and anatomical
record states, [CONSTRUCTION] for what follows by exact counting from the model’s own structure, and
[INTERPRETIVE] for the reading the model proposes upon them.
The three fourteens. [SOURCED] In Plutarch’s account of the Osirian mystery the god is divided into fourteen
members [44, 45]. [CONSTRUCTION] The cuboctahedron has exactly fourteen faces—eight triangular and
six square—and exactly fourteen is the count this paper has already identified with the scattered members.
[INTERPRETIVE] The Vitruvian figure—the body inscribed at once in circle and square, its limbs extended to
the frame—carries fourteen natural division-points at which the body articulates against that frame: the two
hands, two elbows, two shoulders, two hips, two knees, and two feet make the eight limb-stations, and the
crown, the throat, the heart, the navel, the loins, and the soles’ baseline make the six midline-stations. The
three fourteens—Plutarch’s members, the solid’s faces, and the figure’s articulations—are proposed not as
three resemblances but as three renderings of the one structure: the dismembered-and-re-membered body is
the cuboctahedron is the Vitruvian figure.
The 8+6 partition. [CONSTRUCTION] The cuboctahedron’s fourteen faces partition canonically as eight
triangles and six squares, and the figure’s fourteen stations partition, by the same count, into eight bilateral
points (the paired limb-articulations, left and right) and six axial points (the unpaired stations on the midline).
[INTERPRETIVE] This is not a loose pairing of two “fourteens” but a matched partition: the eight triangular
faces are the eight bilateral limb-stations, and the six square faces are the six axial midline-stations. The
triangle, the three-vertex face, falls to the paired limbs that reach outward into relation; the square, the
four-vertex face, falls to the central column that holds the body’s own vertical.
Two integration modes. [INTERPRETIVE] The partition specifies two distinct kinds of shadow-work,
distinguished geometrically rather than rhetorically. The eight bilateral stations are integrated relationally:
each has a mirror-twin across the midline, and the work is the reconciliation of a disowned side with its
acknowledged counterpart—outer shadow-work, the reclaiming of what was projected onto the other. The six
axial stations are integrated internally: each is a single midline structure with no twin, and here virtue and
its shadow are not two organs but two states of one organ—anger as vitality dammed, melancholy as depth
obstructed—so that the work is the recognition that the dark charge was inside the bright chamber all along.

stations, and the internal edges within single ones.

3.1 The hyperboloid as non-compact generator
The single-sheet hyperboloid H , defined by x2 + y2 −z2 = 1, is a connected, non-compact surface of
revolution about the z-axis, with a narrowest circumference—the throat—at z = 0. Endowed with the
metric induced from the ambient Minkowski metric, it carries constant negative Gaussian curvature: it is
a model of the hyperbolic plane. It is unbounded; geodesics that escape the throat toward z →±∞never
return. In rapidity coordinates, where z = sinhθ with θ ∈(−∞,∞) and φ ∈[0,2π) angular, the metric
is ds2 = dθ 2 + cosh2 θ dφ 2: the rapidity coordinate runs without bound, while the angular coordinate is
periodic. Topologically the hyperboloid is a cylinder whose non-compact direction is the rapidity axis. It is
the generating geometry—open, unbounded, never closing on itself.
3.2 The torus as compact quotient
The torus T 2 = S1 ×S1, equivalently the quotient R2/Z2 of the plane by the integer lattice, is by contrast
compact: it has finite surface area and finite enclosed volume, and every geodesic on it remains within a
bounded region, either closing periodically or filling the surface densely. Its defining topological feature is its
two independent loops—one around the major axis, one around the tube—so that functions continued around
either loop return to their starting values up to a definite phase. The torus is therefore the natural setting for
cyclic, recurrent structure: it is the finite geometry, closing back on itself.

4 The Curvature Duality and the Optics of the Self
4.1 Negative and positive curvature: gathering and projecting
The duality between the two manifolds is not merely topological but curvature-categorical. The hyperboloid
is a surface of constant negative Gaussian curvature: at every point it curves away from itself in two
perpendicular directions, the saddle topology of intrinsic concavity. The torus’s outer surface has positive
Gaussian curvature and is intrinsically convex. Together the hyperboloid and the torus’s outer face exhaust
the two non-flat modes of curvature available to a smooth surface in space. And the two modes have opposite
operational character: concavity gathers; convexity projects. This is not metaphor but the operational principle
of lensing—concave elements gather and disperse, convex elements project and focus.
The phenomenological correlate is direct. A concave structure receives, holds, focuses, contains; a convex
structure projects, presents, emits, manifests. The hyperboloidal aspect is the receptive face—the saddle-
throat through which flow is gathered—and the toroidal aspect is the projective face—the convex bulge
through which manifest presence is emitted.
4.2 The exact optical correspondence
The correspondence with classical optics is term-by-term. The hyperboloid corresponds to the concave
lens: a negative-power element that disperses parallel rays, focusing them from a virtual focal point on the
source side—it gathers the field that passes through it. The torus’s outer face corresponds to the convex
lens: a positive-power element that focuses parallel rays to a real focal point on the far side—it projects and
concentrates. The curvature duality of the framework is identical to the curvature duality that organizes the two
classical lens elements: concave/dispersive/receptive and convex/focusing/projective. The hyperboloid–torus
distinction is thus the geometric primitive underlying classical optics itself.

A Technical Appendix: Definitions, Algorithms, and Falsifiable Outputs
This appendix supplies the rigorous substrate the body of the paper draws on. It states the exact definitions,
gives the reproducible algorithm (pure native-integer arithmetic; no floating-point, no external libraries),
records the verified output tables, and enumerates the falsifiable predictions. Its purpose is to make the
load-bearing mathematical claims of the paper independently checkable, and to separate cleanly what is
derived, what is constructed, what is interpretive, and what is empirical.
A.1 Claim-status legend
Every substantive claim in this paper carries one of four statuses. The legend is stated once here and applies
throughout.
[D] Derivation. A statement that follows by exact proof or computation from stated definitions, with no free
parameters. Example: RR(n) = 1 ⇐⇒n is perfect; the additive decomposition of RR; the dual proof of
00 = 1.
[C] Construction. A definitional or architectural choice that is internally consistent and exactly specified, but
is posited rather than forced. Example: the assignment of toroid/hyperboloid to conscious/unconscious;
the cuboctahedron as the discrete identity shell; the mapping of the fourteen faces to the Osirian members.
[I] Interpretation. A reading laid upon a derivation or construction, carrying meaning beyond the formal
content. Example: “the perfect number is the arithmetic image of the observing Self”; the Vitruvian
unnamed-left-hand reading.
[E] Empirical. A claim that makes contact with measured data and could be confirmed or falsified by it.
Example: G = 2/C agreeing with the measured gravitational constant; carbon’s centrality to organic
chemistry. Empirical claims are stated with their residuals and their status (forced vs. fitted) made explicit.
A claim is bulletproof only when it wears the correct uniform: a construction must not be defended as a
derivation, and an interpretation must not be presented as an empirical result. The discipline of this appendix
is that the four are never silently conflated.
