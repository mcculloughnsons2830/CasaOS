## 5. Newsletter #1 — "One Thing Before the Curtain"

**Subject:** The night Anthony Hopkins stopped steering

> Welcome to One Thing Before the Curtain — one piece of late-life wisdom a
> week, from people who earned it.
>
> **This week: Anthony Hopkins, 87.**
>
> In 1975 he woke up at the wheel of a moving car, blackout drunk, and
> understood that the question was no longer if he'd kill someone — only
> when. He asked for help, surrendered, and says an inner voice told him:
> *"It's all over. Now you can start living."* The craving never returned.
>
> The lesson he took from it wasn't about alcohol. It was about control:
> the life you're gripping so tightly was never yours to steer. Plans are
> fine — but the turns that matter won't consult you first.
>
> **The one thing:** pick the outcome you're gripping hardest this week —
> and practice, just once, letting it land where it lands.
>
> Watch the full essay (14 min): [link]
>
> Next week: Clint Eastwood on why he won't "let the old man in."
